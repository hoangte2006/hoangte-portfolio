## Trial Project

This was the earlier trial version of my motorcycle RPM work.
It focused on reading the engine signal reliably and turning it into a stable RPM display.

## What I Used

- Engine pickup signal
- Interrupt-driven RPM counting
- Basic display output
- Recycled components for testing

## What I Learned

This project pushed me to think more carefully about signal design and processing.
I practiced interrupt handling for RPM measurement, learned how to keep the input signal stable, and improved the way I organize and build a system from old materials.

The mechanical assembly took time, but patience and reuse helped me finish the bike installation.

## Build Notes

The dashboard was assembled step by step, then tuned on the bike until the readings became stable.
The result was not perfect, but it was reliable enough for practical use and taught me a lot about real-world embedded design.
