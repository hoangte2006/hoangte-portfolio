## The Book and Its Core Idea

"Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman is a profound book that explores the two systems that drive the way we think:
- **System 1:** Fast, intuitive, and emotional.
- **System 2:** Slower, more deliberative, and more logical.

Reading it helped me recognize these two modes of thinking not just in daily life, but also in my technical work.

## Connection to the RPM Project

While working on the RPM project, I often found myself making quick, intuitive assumptions (System 1) when a bug appeared. For example, I might immediately blame the code when the real issue was a noisy electrical signal.

The book taught me to consciously engage System 2:
1.  **Stop and Question:** Instead of jumping to conclusions, I started to pause and list all possible causes for a problem.
2.  **Deliberate Analysis:** I would methodically test each hypothesis, from the physical hardware to the software logic, rather than just tweaking the code and hoping for the best.
3.  **Recognizing Biases:** I became aware of "confirmation bias" – the tendency to look for evidence that confirms my initial guess. This helped me stay objective during debugging.

This structured, slower approach ultimately helped me solve the complex signal processing issues in the RPM project more effectively.

## Broader Impact

Beyond a single project, this book has given me a framework to view life's problems with more richness and logic. It's a powerful reminder that our first instincts aren't always right and that taking the time to think slowly and deliberately is a superpower in engineering and beyond.
